My Wellness Toolkit!

Do you find yourself spending excessive amounts of time on social media to distract yourself from life's hardships?

Do you feel like your self-worth is becoming attached to the content you put out and consume on a daily basis?

I'm here to tell you not to worry! You're not alone.

Plenty of us have felt the same things.

My Experiences

Social Media

Spending less time outdoors in the past couple years developed a bad habit of doomscrolling within me. I found myself spending hours at night scrolling and refreshing my Twitter feed, consuming content until I passed out.

Social Media's Downsides

Increased Instability

Recent studies referenced by The National Center for Health Research suggest that people who frequently use social media feel less happy with life. It can severely interfere with your dopamine levels.

How to Help Yourself!

Unplugging

Connecting myself with the real world helped immensely. Taking time off of the internet to read more books, play more music, and talk to my family members more made me feel less paranoid about things and lowered my FOMO(fear of missing out) significantly.

Connecting With Others

Humans Need Connection

Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs places love and belonging as our third most basic need. Belongingness refers to an emotional need for interpersonal relationships and connections.

Make sure to spend time with those you care about! Reach out to people - you'd be surprised how often people want to talk but also feel awkward texting first.

Take Care of Your Body

Sleep!

As tempting as it may be to stay up to "degen hours", your body needs adequate sleep. Without it, you're more likely to feel worse overall: you're more prone to mood swings, your memory can be spotty, and you can struggle to focus, among other things.

Make "Me" Time

Keep In Touch With Yourself

As cliche as the phrase "it's okay not to be okay" is, it's the truth! Make sure you take time for yourself to check in with your emotional and mental state. Give yourself time to process things(and sometimes, it's okay to just make time to do nothing! Treat yourself <3).
